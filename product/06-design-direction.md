# Design Direction

## Visual Philosophy

The visual system is dark, cinematic, and restrained.

The goal is to elevate the photography — not compete with it.

The interface should feel:

- Premium
- Quiet
- Intentional
- Confident

No decorative noise.
No unnecessary UI.
No bright distractions.

---

## Core Principles

1. Photography is the hero.
2. UI should disappear.
3. Motion should feel subtle and deliberate.
4. Typography should feel editorial, not corporate.
5. Negative space is a feature, not wasted space.

---

## Tone

The brand should feel:

- Minimal
- Modern
- Trustworthy
- Artistic without being trendy

Avoid:

- Loud accent colors
- Heavy shadows
- Overuse of glassmorphism
- Overly complex animations

---

## Color System

Primary Background:
- Deep charcoal / near black

Secondary Background:
- Slightly lighter gray for layering

Text:
- Soft white (not pure white)
- Muted gray for secondary content

Accent:
- Extremely restrained
- Used only for interaction states

---

## Typography

Primary:
- Clean sans-serif (e.g., Inter)

Styling:
- Tight letter spacing
- Medium weight headings
- Generous line-height for body text

Typography should feel editorial — not startup SaaS.

---

## Motion System

Motion should feel:

- Subtle
- Eased
- Slow enough to feel intentional
- Fast enough to not frustrate

No bounce.
No playful transitions.
No exaggerated effects.

Gallery transitions should feel cinematic — fade + slight scale.

---

## Gallery Experience

- Grid layout.
- Strong spacing.
- Large image previews.
- Lazy loading.
- Soft fade-in for image reveal.
- Blur preview before password unlock.

The gallery should feel like entering a private screening room.

---

## Emotional Goal

The system should make clients feel:

- Their work is treated with care.
- Their memories are presented professionally.
- They are receiving something curated.

The system should make the photographer feel:

- Professional.
- Established.
- Legitimate.
